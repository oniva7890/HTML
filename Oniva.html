Honey bees:

Honey bees aresocial insects belonging to the genus Apis , known for living in large, complex colonies and producing honey.

1. The Colony Hierarchy

● Workers: Thousands of sterile females that do all the labor—cleaning, feeding larvae, building wax combs, guarding the entrance, and foraging for nectar and pollen.

,

● Drones: Several hundred males whose sole purpose is to mate with a new queen. They do not have stingers and are often expelled from the hive before winter to conserve food.

2. Life Cycle & Development

Honey bees undergo a four-stage life cycle called complete metamorphosis :

1.Egg: Laid by the queen in hexagonal wax cells.

2.Pupa: The cell is capped with wax while the bee develops wings, legs, and eyes.

Adult: The bee chews its way out. Total development time varies: 16 days for queens, 21 days for workers, and 24 days for drones.

3. Fascinating Facts

● Communication: Bees use a "waggle dance"—a figure-eight movement—to tell others the exact direction and distance to a rich food source.

● Anatomy: They have five eyes (two large compound eyes and three small light-detecting ocelli) and can see ultraviolet light, helping them spot patterns on flowers that humans cannot see.

● Production: A single worker bee produces only about 1/12th of a teaspoon of honey in her lifetime.

● Winter Survival: They do not hibernate; instead, they cluster around the queen and shiver their wing muscles to keep the hive warm.

4. Products & Benefits

Beyond pollination, humans harvest several products from hives, including honey, beeswax (used in cosmetics and candles), propolis (a resin used as a sealant), and royal jelly.
